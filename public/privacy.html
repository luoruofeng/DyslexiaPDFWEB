本地处理优先： One-PDF 是一款专注于 PDF 阅读和处理的工具应用。您在使用本应用导入、阅读、编辑或导出的所有 PDF 文档，默认均在您的设备本地进行处理。我们不会主动将您的文档内容上传至我们的服务器，也不会收集或存储您的任何个人文档内容。

本应用可能集成了由第三方提供的服务（例如 Apple 的原生框架或其他基础服务）。这些第三方服务可能会根据其各自的隐私政策收集部分匿名信息。我们承诺只在必要范围内使用安全可靠的第三方服务，以保障应用的核心体验。

3. 数据安全

我们采取合理且必要的安全措施来保护您的信息免受未经授权的访问、使用或泄露。由于文档主要在本地处理，您设备系统自带的安全机制（如设备密码、Face ID 或 Touch ID）将直接作为保护您文档安全的第一道防线。

  • 文件和文件夹权限： 用于读取您选择的 PDF 文件以及保存处理后的文件。
  • 网络权限： 仅用于应用内的部分可选联网功能（如有）或崩溃日志收集。

您可以在设备的系统设置中随时撤销上述权限，但这可能会影响应用的部分功能。

Last Updated: April 15, 2026

Thank you for using One-PDF ("the App"). We highly value your privacy and are committed to protecting your personal information. This Privacy Policy is designed to explain how we collect, use, and safeguard your information when you use the App, fulfilling App Store review requirements and keeping users informed.

1. Information Collection and Use

Local Processing Priority: One-PDF is a tool app focused on PDF reading and processing. All PDF documents you import, read, edit, or export using the App are processed locally on your device by default. We do not proactively upload your document content to our servers, nor do we collect or store any of your personal document content.

Device and Usage Data: To improve app performance and user experience, we may collect anonymous, non-personally identifiable crash reports and basic usage statistics. This data is used solely for diagnosing issues and optimizing the app, and cannot be used to identify you personally.

The App may integrate services provided by third parties (such as Apple's native frameworks or other foundational services). These third-party services may collect certain anonymous information in accordance with their respective privacy policies. We commit to using secure and reliable third-party services only to the extent necessary to ensure the core experience of the app.

3. Data Security

We take reasonable and necessary security measures to protect your information from unauthorized access, use, or disclosure. Since documents are primarily processed locally, the built-in security mechanisms of your device's operating system (such as device passcode, Face ID, or Touch ID) serve directly as the first line of defense for your document security.

While providing services, the App may request the following device permissions from you:
